Q1. A 68-year-old male admitted with pneumonia suddenly becomes restless. BP 88/54, HR 124, RR 30, SpO₂ 89% on room air. What is the nurse’s PRIORITY action?



Trainer Review:
Fail-fast hypoxia + hypotension. ABC → Airway/Breathing before notification or documentation.
Q2. A postoperative patient reports sudden chest pain and shortness of breath. SpO₂ is 86%. What should the nurse do FIRST?



Trainer Review:
SCFHS prioritizes immediate oxygenation before diagnostics or notification.
Q3. A diabetic patient is confused and diaphoretic. Capillary glucose is 42 mg/dL. What is the MOST appropriate action?



Trainer Review:
Life-threatening hypoglycemia → treat immediately. Assessment or documentation = delay trap.
Q4. A patient with sepsis has BP 90/50, HR 118, urine output 15 mL/hr. What does the nurse do FIRST?



Trainer Review:
Circulation failure → perfusion before protocol or reporting.
Q5. A patient suddenly becomes unresponsive. No pulse is felt. What is the nurse’s IMMEDIATE action?



Trainer Review:
SCFHS fail-fast: action before activation or documentation.
Q6. A patient receiving IV potassium reports burning at the IV site. What should the nurse do FIRST?



Trainer Review:
Medication safety → stop before adjusting or documenting.
Q7. A confused elderly patient tries to climb out of bed. What is the BEST nursing action?



Trainer Review:
Least restrictive + immediate safety = SCFHS ethics tie-breaker.
Q8. A patient’s respiratory rate drops to 8/min after opioid administration. What is the PRIORITY action?



Trainer Review:
Fail-fast respiratory depression → reversal before observation.
Q9. A patient reports sudden severe headache and unequal pupils. What should the nurse do FIRST?



Trainer Review:
Neurological fail-fast → rapid escalation.
Q10. A patient receiving blood transfusion develops fever and chills. What is the FIRST nursing action?



Trainer Review:
Blood reaction = immediate stop. Classic SCFHS fail-fast.
